## Claims reviewed

1. Over 1,900 U.S. four-year universities (over 80%) remain test-optional or test-free for Fall 2024 and 2025 cycles.
2. Harvard, Yale, Dartmouth, Brown, MIT, Stanford, Cornell, and UT Austin have reinstated SAT or ACT requirements for Fall 2025 or 2026 admissions cycles.
3. Public university systems in Florida and Georgia are requiring standardized test scores for admission.
4. Proponents of reinstating test requirements argue that scores help identify talented students from diverse backgrounds, promote meritocracy, and provide a reliable metric to differentiate applicants amid widespread grade inflation.
5. The SAT shifted to a digital, adaptive format in 2024.
6. The ACT announced plans to make its science section optional and separate from the composite score.
7. In K-12 education, states are moving away from single, high-stakes end-of-year exams toward through-year assessments and performance-based models.

## Recommended edits for editor

- **Soften** the claim about 1,900 universities remaining test-optional for Fall 2024 and 2025. Clarify that while over 1,900 schools were test-optional for Fall 2024, the number is slightly lower (1,825+) for Fall 2025, though it still represents over 80% of U.S. four-year universities.
- **Add attribution** to FairTest for the statistics on test-optional universities.
- **Add attribution** to the proponents' arguments for reinstating test requirements (e.g., "according to research cited by institutions like Harvard and MIT").

## Residual risks

- The exact number of test-optional schools for Fall 2025 may fluctuate as more universities announce their policies closer to the admissions cycle.
- The implementation details and timelines for K-12 assessment reforms vary significantly by state, so broad generalizations may not apply to every district.
